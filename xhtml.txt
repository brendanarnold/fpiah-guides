<!DOCTYPE html PUBLIC "-//W3C//DTD XHTML 1.0 Transitional//EN" "DTD/xhtml1-transitional.dtd">

<!-- Don't try to understand it until much later, just make sure it at the top of all your webpages. -->

<!-- So you want to write a webpage properly?  Copy and paste this text
into a  file callled index.html and open it with your web-browser
(Internet Explorer, Netscape whatever) You should see a web-page.

Note: None of the text between <!-- and --> markers are seen, these are
      comments and are invisible to the web-browser.  -->


<html>

<!-- All content between the 'head' tags (i.e. from <head> to </head>)
is used by the browser and does not actually appear on the webpage. It
is important. -->

<head>

<!-- Text in the 'title' tags is generally used by the browser (look at
the very top of the screen) and search engines to index your webpage -->

<title>YOUR WEBPAGE TITLE HERE</title>

</head>

<!-- Content between the 'body' tags appears on the webpage -->

<body>

<!-- 'h1' stands for header level 1. There should be one h1 per webpage.
We can change how h1 text looks by using Style Sheets (see other FPIAH
guide to CSS) -->

<h1>My First Webpage</h1>

<p>My first paragraph of webpage text.</p>

<p>I am <em>really</em> excited to be using <abbr title="eXtensible Hypertext Markup Language">XHTML</abbr> to markup my first webpage.</p>


</body>
</html>
